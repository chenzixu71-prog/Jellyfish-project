# Live-Action Micro-Expression Prompt Bank

Use these prompts for realistic AI short drama acting. Default intensity is restrained. Avoid exaggerated emotion unless the script demands melodrama.

## Intensity Scale

1. barely visible: useful for hidden emotion, suspense, suppressed anger
2. subtle but readable: default for close-ups
3. clear drama performance: use for key beats
4. heightened: use rarely for cliffhangers
5. exaggerated: avoid for live-action realistic short drama unless requested

## Baseline Acting Style

Add to prompts:
`realistic live-action performance, restrained facial expression, natural skin texture, subtle eye movement, no exaggerated grimace, no theatrical overacting`

## Core Micro-Expressions

Controlled anger:
- `intensity 2, eyes steady but colder, jaw lightly tightened, lips pressed for a moment, shoulders still`
- `intensity 3, slight nostril tension, gaze fixed, lower eyelids tense, breath held before speaking`

Suppressed hurt:
- `intensity 2, eyes glisten slightly, mouth relaxes then closes, chin lowers a few degrees, tries not to react`
- `intensity 3, brief swallow, eyes look away for half a second, lips tremble almost imperceptibly`

Quiet suspicion:
- `intensity 2, eyes narrow slightly, head tilts a few degrees, lips remain neutral, attention sharpens`
- `intensity 3, gaze moves from object to face, brow tightens lightly, silence before response`

Hidden panic:
- `intensity 2, pupils alert, breath becomes shallow, smile freezes for a beat`
- `intensity 3, eyes widen slightly, throat swallows, fingers stop moving, face tries to stay calm`

Cold confidence:
- `intensity 2, relaxed eyelids, faint controlled smile, steady posture, slow blink`
- `intensity 3, subtle half-smile, chin lifts slightly, gaze does not retreat`

Realization:
- `intensity 2, eyes refocus, lips part slightly, body becomes still`
- `intensity 3, brows lift then settle, breath catches, gaze locks onto the clue`

Guilt:
- `intensity 2, gaze drops briefly, mouth tightens at one corner, shoulders close inward`
- `intensity 3, avoids eye contact, small swallow, fingers tense`

Tender restraint:
- `intensity 2, eyes soften, mouth almost smiles, breath slows, posture leans forward slightly`
- `intensity 3, warm but contained gaze, faint smile, pauses before touching or speaking`

Disgust hidden under politeness:
- `intensity 2, smile remains but eyes lose warmth, upper lip tightens slightly`
- `intensity 3, controlled smile fades for one beat, gaze measures the other person`

Shock without overacting:
- `intensity 2, blink stops, lips part slightly, shoulders freeze`
- `intensity 3, eyes widen moderately, breath catches, body stills before response`

## Character-Specific Variation

For a calm protagonist:
- favor stillness, steady gaze, small jaw tension, low voice.

For a wounded protagonist:
- favor brief eye avoidance, swallowed emotion, controlled breathing.

For a manipulative antagonist:
- favor polite smile, cold eyes, delayed blink, precise posture.

For a comic friend:
- allow more eyebrow and mouth movement, but keep within intensity 3 for realistic style.

## Negative Prompt Additions

Use when the model overacts:
`no exaggerated crying, no wide-open screaming mouth, no cartoon expression, no melodramatic grimace, no distorted face, no unnatural smile, no beauty filter, no plastic skin`
