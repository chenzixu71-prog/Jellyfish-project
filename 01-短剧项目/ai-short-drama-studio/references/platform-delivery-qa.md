# Voice, Subtitle, Edit, and Delivery QA

## Voice Profiles

For each recurring character define:
- voice age
- pitch range
- speaking speed
- emotional floor
- emotional ceiling
- signature pause
- forbidden tone

Example:
`FL01: female, 26-28, clear low-mid pitch, controlled pace, emotional floor calm, ceiling restrained anger, often pauses before exposing a lie, avoid childish or overly sweet tone.`

## Subtitle Rules

- Match subtitles to final audio, not the draft script.
- Keep each subtitle short enough for mobile reading.
- Use punctuation to support rhythm, not to decorate.
- Avoid covering faces, hands, phone screens, or key evidence props.
- Verify names, relationship titles, and plot clues.

## Edit QA

Check each episode:
- first 3 seconds contain conflict or curiosity
- no long exposition before the hook
- each scene has visible power tension
- every episode ends with a cliffhanger or reversal
- character face does not drift
- wardrobe continuity holds
- expression is readable but not exaggerated
- voice matches character
- subtitles match audio
- music does not bury dialogue
- no obvious generated-video artifacts in key close-ups

## Platform Package

Prepare:
- title options
- cover prompt or cover image
- short synopsis
- episode list
- tags/keywords
- copyright ownership notes
- tool chain notes
- compliance risk notes
- sample screenshots
- delivery folder structure

## Compliance Notes

Treat these as risk checks, not legal advice:
- avoid unauthorized celebrity likeness
- avoid copyrighted music or images without license
- avoid copying a known short drama plot beat-for-beat
- avoid sensitive topics that platform rules may restrict
- keep project files proving original script, generated assets, and edit decisions
